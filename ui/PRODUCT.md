# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mixed product teams — PMs, designers, and engineers — brainstorming together in one session, alongside the agent. They arrive mid-thought, share vocabulary loosely (a PM and an engineer name the same idea differently), and want to externalize and shape ideas fast. The agent (a Codex plugin) is a participant in the room, not a tool behind a send button. Sessions live on a hosted URL anyone on the team can open.

## Product Purpose

barbequeue moves brainstorming from human-to-agent (1:1, one person prompting a model) to **humans-to-agent**: several people and the agent thinking in the same chat session. The product is that shared session, hosted at a URL. Success looks like a team that would otherwise have brainstormed in a doc or a meeting choosing to do it here instead — because the agent participating live, with everyone watching and building on each other, produces better ideas faster. The first surface under `/ui` is the chat itself; its visual identity is what we are shaping.

## Brand Personality

Warm workshop. The name is the thesis: a barbecue is communal, unhurried, a little hot — people gather around it and something gets cooked together. Ideas get grilled. The voice is confident and human, never corporate and never cute. Three words: **communal, warm, sharp**. The warmth is real (heat, ember, gathering) but grown-up — it carries through accent color, type, and texture, not through mascots or rounded-everything friendliness.

## Anti-references

- **Slack / Discord** — chrome-heavy team chat: persistent sidebars, presence dots everywhere, reaction clutter. barbequeue is a focused room, not a workspace shell.
- **Corporate SaaS** — navy-and-blue, safe rounded cards, stock imagery, the default B2B look. No.
- **Playful / cartoonish** — mascots, bouncy elastic motion, emoji-forward, rounded everything. The warmth is mature, not childish.
- **Generic AI chat** — the ChatGPT/Claude.ai clone (centered column, gray bubbles, sparkle icon). Multi-human attribution and the workshop feel must make this read as something else entirely.
- **The cream/sand/terracotta AI default** — "warm workshop" must NOT become a beige near-white body. Warmth lives in ember accents and type, against a committed dark or true-neutral ground.

## Design Principles

- **The agent is in the room.** It's a participant with presence and voice, not a response that appears below a prompt box. Attribution and turn-taking treat it as a peer.
- **Many voices, one fire.** Multi-human attribution must be legible at a glance without Slack-style chrome. Who said what, and what the group is converging on, should be readable instantly.
- **Warmth is earned, not painted on.** Heat comes from ember accent, confident type (Helvetica Now Display), and texture — never from a beige background or friendly rounding.
- **A room to think in, not a tool to operate.** Low chrome, generous space for ideas, minimal persistent UI. The content (the thinking) is the interface.
- **Cooked, not raw.** Ideas have state — fresh, being worked, settled. The interface can reflect that an idea is "on the grill" vs. done.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1, large/UI text ≥3:1 — including on the dark/ember ground where contrast is easiest to lose. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation. Author attribution must never rely on color alone (name/avatar shape carries it too), so the multi-human distinction survives color-blindness.
