# Barbequeue ElevenLabs Video Prompt

Use this as the master prompt for ElevenLabs Image & Video / ElevenCreative. After landing screenshots are available, upload them as visual references and replace the bracketed reference notes in the visual style section.

## Recommended Settings

- Format: 16:9 widescreen
- Duration: 85-90 seconds
- Output: polished product-pitch video, stage-deck style, not a literal app walkthrough
- Voice: confident founder voice, warm but direct, clear pacing, no hype cadence
- Visual language: dark product surface, typographic motion, geometric consensus animation
- Motion: controlled, deliberate, stateful; each transition explains the product mechanism

## Master Prompt

Create a 90-second cinematic product-pitch video for Barbequeue, a multiplayer AI grilling session for software teams. The video should feel like a premium scroll-driven landing page used as a pitch deck: full-viewport sections, keyboard-advanced beats, precise typography, dark warm-charcoal surfaces, restrained ember accents, and structured product motion.

The central story is: one person starts with an idea, AI asks sharp grilling questions, the whole team contributes, objections are surfaced and resolved, many inputs converge into one answer candidate, and the team leaves with documented alignment.

Use the uploaded landing-page screenshots as the primary visual reference for layout, typography, spacing, logo treatment, and color behavior. Preserve the same brand direction exactly: Charcoal & Ember. The environment is dark warm charcoal, not black and not white. Ember is used only for brand, primary actions, focus, and the final CTA. Functional colors appear only when they carry product meaning: green for accepted consensus, hot red for unresolved objection, cool blue for clarification. Do not use cream, sand, generic SaaS gradients, literal fire imagery, stock photos, decorative flames, chat bubbles, floating card stacks, or generic AI dashboard visuals.

Visual vocabulary: use the Barbequeue logo marks, simple circles and one square, as the animation language. Circles represent people and contributions. Forms appear, gather, object, clarify, and resolve. The video must never look like a Slack chat or a meeting tool. It should look like a structured consensus workflow: Grilling Question -> Contributions -> Answer Candidate -> Session Consensus -> Documentation Change.

Tone: sharp, spirited, deliberate. The product should feel serious enough for engineering decisions, but not corporate or bureaucratic. The energy comes from motion, contrast, and the reveal of consensus, not from decoration.

## Scene Plan

### 1. Hook / Hero, 0-12s

Visual:
Start on a dark warm-charcoal full-screen hero. The Barbequeue wordmark is precise and still. The logo marks breathe subtly: circle, circle, circle, square. Tagline appears: "Nobody grills alone." Keep the frame minimal, spacious, and typographic. Hint at the next section below the fold as if this is a scroll-driven landing page.

Voiceover:
"Some of the best ideas in human history were born around a fire - people, together, throwing ideas into the flames. AI gave us a bigger fire. But it took away the people."

### 2. Problem / Single-Player AI, 12-24s

Visual:
Transition downward into an isolated input line on a dark raised surface. It feels lonely: one active cursor in ember, no surrounding workflow, no team structure, no consensus board. The space around it is intentionally empty. Do not show a generic chatbot interface.

Voiceover:
"Working through an idea with AI is usually private - you, alone in a chat window. The grill sharpens that plan brilliantly. But the hard part was never the idea - it's getting the team to agree."

### 3. Reveal / Barbequeue Starts, 24-34s

Visual:
The frame opens into a structured product surface. One ember action appears: "Light the grill." A host drops in an idea. A clean invite link appears and sends out small geometric marks. The marks should echo the logo rather than look like avatars.

Voiceover:
"So we built Barbequeue. One person lights the grill, drops in an idea, and shares a link."

### 4. Team Joins / Grilling Question, 34-45s

Visual:
Single circles arrive one by one across the charcoal field, forming a room. A central thin structural frame rises into place. Inside it, show one large Grilling Question:
"What has to be true before this plan is safe to ship?"

Voiceover:
"Everyone opens the same page. The AI asks the grilling questions -"

### 5. Contributions Arrive, 45-57s

Visual:
Short contributions appear around the question, staggered and distinct. They are spatial fragments, not chat bubbles. Example fragments:
"Document the fallback path"
"Clarify who owns rollout"
"Risk: participants may miss context"
"Keep raw repo files local"

Use cool blue for one clarifying contribution and hot red for one objection. Keep most contributions muted ink. The objection can briefly flare with a restrained red glow.

Voiceover:
"- and the whole team contributes. Together."

### 6. Mediation / Convergence, 57-68s

Visual:
The AI mediation begins. Contributions are compared, reordered, and pulled inward. The red objection remains visible until the answer candidate accounts for it. The movement should feel like synthesis, not magic: lines, grouping, compression, and resolution. At center, the Answer Candidate forms:
"Ship only after rollout ownership, fallback behavior, and participant context are documented."

Voiceover:
"Then it does the real work. It mediates - compares every contribution, amplifies the best, and grills the room..."

### 7. Consensus Hold, 68-72s

Visual:
Hold a silent beat. The scattered contributions resolve into one clear Answer Candidate. The objection fades only after it is addressed. A green accepted consensus state lands after the room is aligned. This is the most important visual moment: many human inputs become one shared decision.

Voiceover:
"...until you land on one."

### 8. Outcome, 72-82s

Visual:
Show two structured output bands, not floating marketing cards:
"Documentation Change ready for host review"
"Session Record the team can trust"

The accepted answer remains visible as the source of both outputs. Show that the result is documented and aligned, not merely summarized.

Voiceover:
"You don't just leave with a sharper idea - documented, ready. You leave as a team that finally agrees on it. The idea is what you cooked. The alignment is why you lit the fire."

### 9. CTA, 82-90s

Visual:
Final full-screen charcoal CTA. Large type:
"AI · powered by · HI"

Below it, show:
"Try it on the Codex marketplace"
"Tell us what you think"

Use ember only for the primary CTA and focus. Add a QR-code placeholder if no real QR code asset is uploaded. End cleanly on the Barbequeue wordmark and the logo marks.

Voiceover:
"This is Artificial Intelligence - powered by Human Intelligence. Barbequeue is live on the Codex marketplace. Go grill something with your team - and tell us what you think. Nobody grills alone. HI."

## Negative Prompt

Do not show literal fire, campfires, flames, stock office workers, robots, generic AI brains, beige SaaS websites, purple gradients, cream backgrounds, glossy glassmorphism, floating feature cards, chat bubbles, Slack-style message feeds, crowded enterprise dashboards, smiling avatar grids, hand-drawn mascots, or decorative color that does not carry meaning.

Do not make the product look finished as a generic dashboard. Make it feel like a controlled landing-page pitch deck that explains the mechanism through motion.
