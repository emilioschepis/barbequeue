Some of the best ideas in human history were born around a fire: people together, throwing ideas into the flames.

AI gave us a bigger fire.

But it took away the people.

Working through an idea with AI is usually private: you, alone in a chat window.

The grill can sharpen that plan brilliantly.

But the hard part was never just the idea.

The hard part is getting the team to agree.

So we built Barbequeue.

One person lights the grill, drops in an idea, and shares a link.

Everyone opens the same page.

The AI asks the grilling questions, and the whole team contributes. Together.

Then it does the real work.

It mediates.

It compares every contribution, amplifies the best parts, and keeps grilling the room until objections are resolved.

Many human inputs become one shared decision.

You do not just leave with a sharper idea.

You leave with the record: accepted outcomes, dismissed objections, and a host-reviewed documentation change ready for the repo.

The idea is what you cooked.

The alignment is why you lit the fire.

Grill the plan. Leave with the record.
